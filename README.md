# Stock Market

## Overview
Synthetic daily stock data (2018–2026) for major tech companies (AAPL, MSFT, AMZN, GOOGL, META, TSLA, NFLX).  
Ideal for Tableau visualizations, portfolio analysis, or time-series experiments.

## Dataset Structure
- **Columns** (14):  
  Date, Stock_Symbol, Company_Name, Sector, Market, Country, Trading_Day,  
  Open_Price, High_Price, Low_Price, Close_Price, Volume, Market_Cap_Bn, Daily_Return_%

- Rows: ~3,000  
- No missing values

## Key Insights
- Average daily return: ~0.02%  
- Top performers (avg daily return): NFLX (0.19%), TSLA (0.14%), META (0.08%)  
- Worst: AAPL (-0.19%), MSFT (-0.15%)  
- Highest market cap recorded: ~$2,500 Bn  
- Includes weekends (synthetic data)

## Use Cases
- Price & return trend visualization  
- Sector / symbol comparison  
- Volatility & correlation analysis  
- Educational demos & ML forecasting practice

## Future Improvements

- Replace with real historical data

## Key Insights :-

- Tesla was the undisputed leader in stock price growth until mid-2021, peaking above 600K (pre-split adjusted), but suffered the sharpest decline post-2022, dropping over 70% by 2025.
- Meta Platforms experienced the most dramatic fall among FAANG stocks – from ~550K in 2021 to just 33,560 by 2025 (highlighting severe underperformance in the Meta verse era).
- Amazon consistently held the highest average trading volume (~1.6B shares), proving it remains the most actively traded tech stock over the period.
- E-Commerce sector (led by Amazon) dominates total trading volume with over 3.09 trillion shares traded (2018–2025), slightly ahead of Automotive (Tesla-dominated).
- Netflix has the lowest trading volume among the group (~1.2B), yet maintained relatively stable stock price compared to Meta and Tesla.
- Post-2022 market correction hit growth stocks hard – all lines converge downward sharply after 2024, with Tesla and Meta showing maximum volatility.
- Apple & Microsoft proved to be the most resilient, maintaining relatively higher and stable prices throughout the bear market of 2023–2025.
